when it mattered.

Four material moments—not every version number. Each is linked to its source and translated into the question a potential wearer should ask.

Iconic square-frame AI glasses for everyday use.

27 SEP 2023

Capture became easier to notice.

Meta launched the next Ray-Ban generation with a larger, more visible capture LED intended to signal when people are being photographed, filmed, or livestreamed.

PROTECTION STRENGTHENED

Prisim’s read: This improves the bystander signal—but it only works if the light stays visible and people recognise what it means.

Meta announcement ↗
02 OCT 2024

Looking became an AI input.

Meta confirmed that images or video a wearer shares with Meta AI for analysis may be used to improve its AI, depending on the product terms and location.

DATA USE EXPANDED

Prisim’s read: A camera is no longer only a capture tool when visual AI is enabled. The key decision is when a view becomes an AI prompt.

TechCrunch reporting ↗Meta system card ↗
29 APR 2025

AI settings moved toward default-on.

Meta notified Ray-Ban Meta owners of policy changes around AI features and the use of voice interactions to improve its products, as reported by TechCrunch.

PRIVACY WATCH

Prisim’s read: More useful voice AI should come with an especially clear explanation of retention, review, and the controls a wearer can actually change.

TechCrunch reporting ↗
08 JUL 2026

LED tampering now disables the camera.

Meta says it is updating its AI glasses so the camera is disabled if the capture LED is physically tampered with or destroyed, extending the earlier blocked-LED safeguard.

PROTECTION STRENGTHENED

Prisim’s read: This responds directly to a real weakness in a bystander-protection control. It is meaningful because it makes the safeguard harder to bypass.
